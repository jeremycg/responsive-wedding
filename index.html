Sangeet & Mehndi

Wednesday July 6th 2016
5:30 PM
Cheema Household
6250 Moretta Drive, Niagara Falls, ON

The sangeet and mehndi is a traditional pre-wedding festivity in Punjabi culture. Both men and women are welcome to attend from both sides. It is basically a big party at my parents’ house to kick things off.

There will be lots of singing, dancing, drinking, and even more food (so come hungry!).

In terms of attire, this is a casual affair: think summer evening BBQ.

For those of you driving in, the best parking option will be street parking around my parents' neighbourhood.

Ceremony/Anand Karaj

Friday July 8th 2016
9:30 AM
Halton Sikh Cultural Association
2403 Khalsa Gate, Oakville, ON

Our wedding ceremony (also known as the Anand Karaj) will take place at the Sikh gurdwara in Oakville.

The morning will start off with snacks and tea (don’t ever question if there will be food at an Indian wedding). The bride and groom’s families generally will have a jovial meeting outside the temple during this time.

The actual ceremony will be about 30-40 minutes in length. During this portion of the ceremony men and women are expected to sit on opposite sides of the hall.

After the ceremony, langar (lunch) will be served downstairs. It is customary for everyone to sit on the ground while langar is served and eaten.

In terms of timing, you should plan to be at the temple until about noon.

In terms of attire, you should feel comfortable sitting down on the ground for a few hours in whatever you choose to wear. Everyone is expected to cover their heads as well. For men, these head coverings will be provided at the gurdwara and for the ladies, I suggest bringing a scarf/shawl to cover your head with during the ceremony. For the ladies , please keep in mind the sitting situation - ie. you probably won’t feel comfortable sitting down in a short skirt. I usually recommend a long skirt or pants and a longer top.

For those of you driving in, there is a large parking lot at the gurdwara that should accommodate everyone.
